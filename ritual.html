The Ritual

Nothing needs to happen
immediately.

This plant does not respond to urgency. It grows quietly, out of sight, before it rewards you.

Before you plant

If you want, place the cutting somewhere visible for one day. Let it become familiar before you ask anything of it.

Planting

  • Fill a small pot with the soil provided (or any fast-draining succulent soil).
  • Make a small hole and place the cutting in the soil.
  • Press the soil gently so it can stand. Don’t pack it hard.

Watering

Use very little water. For the first watering, a small splash is enough. After that, only water when the soil is fully dry.

If you forget to water it, it will forgive you.
If you water it too often, it won’t.

Light (UK seasons)

  • Summer: bright, indirect light. Keep away from harsh direct sun.
  • Winter: place near a window or windowsill for maximum daylight.

What to expect

Roots form quietly, out of sight. You may see nothing for weeks — that is normal.

  • Weeks 1–4: nothing obvious is fine.
  • Weeks 4–8: gentle resistance when you tug can indicate rooting.
  • Later: new growth appears when it’s ready, not when you check.

Seaweed tonic

Once a month, add a tiny amount of tonic to your watering. More is not better. Small is enough.

If something feels “wrong”

  • Soft or mushy base: too much water. Let the soil dry completely and reduce watering.
  • No visible change: often just low winter light. Move it closer to a window and wait.
  • Dry edge: normal. This is a plant that prefers being left alone.

You are not behind. You are simply early.
